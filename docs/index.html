While other datasets are yet to come, I am working with forest inventory data plus matching lidar from Paisagens Sustentáveis and Marcus Vinicio Neves d’Oliveira in Brazil.

Cleaning data

Inventory datasets were compiled and standardised. Taxonomic errors were corrected using the Taxonomic Name Resolution Service v5.0 (https://tnrs.biendata.org/).

All inventories matching with lidar (50plots/tansects in 50 locations) were selected, but some data were excluded sequentially:

  • 50 inventory transects (i.e., 500 meter transects in Paisagen Sustentáveis).

  • 50 re-sampled inventory plots. I selected most recent plots (not always) with the least time difference from its lidar survey.

  • 50 plots which had more than half of the trees dead and less than 10 individuals.

  • 50 dead individuals were removed. (I may add them later into biomass estimations, 50 out of 50 (50%) dead trees have DBH measurements).

  • 50 lianas.

  • 50 trees without DBH measurement.

Finally, what’s left for the analysis are 50 individuals in 50 plots in 50 locations:

DBH (Diameter at Breast Height)

Plots from Paisagens Sustentáveis have different DBH threshold in their plots. Many of them, measured trees >35cm DBH in the whole plot area and >10cm in a sub-plot area. Sub-plots vary in size across locations, for instance, a 10m x 50m subplot within a 50m x 50m plot. Therefore, some expansion factors will be applied when calculating plot AGB.

Project description

whose model parameters a and b vary with forest types. Basal Area (BA) and wood density (ρ) will be coupled as sub-models to develop a generalised model that can be applicable across different forest types:

\[ ACD_{plot} = a · TCH^d · BA^c · ρ^b; \] ACD will be calculated using field plot data by summing above-ground biomass (AGB) for each tree (Chave et al., 2014) and then multiplying by 0.47, which is the conversion factor for carbon content (Martin & Thomas, 2011).

LiDAR data will be used to calculate TCH, while BA and ρ are derived from field plot data. This modelling approach will provide a better understanding on how BA and ρ affect ACD.
